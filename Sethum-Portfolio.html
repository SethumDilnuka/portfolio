Featured Projects

HandPilot Car

"Driving Independence: The HandPilot Car revolutionizes mobility for the disabled with ergonomic, hand-controlled navigation."

Accessible Mobility Embedded Systems Patent #20087

The HandPilot Car is an innovative, hand-controlled vehicle designed to provide independent mobility for individuals with leg disabilities. It features ergonomic controls for acceleration, braking, and steering, ensuring a safe and user-friendly experience.

  • Ergonomic controls
  • Safety-certified
  • Award-winning design

Impact: Recognized nationally and patented (SL Registration No. 20087)

Smart Cleaning Robot

"Clean Smarter: An autonomous cleaning robot that navigates your space with precision and ease."

IoT Automation Embedded Systems

This WiFi-controlled autonomous cleaning robot uses Arduino, motor drivers, and sensors for obstacle detection and navigation. It features a mobile app for control and a 3D-printed chassis, making it a versatile and efficient cleaning solution.

  • Mobile app control
  • Sensor navigation
  • 3D-printed chassis

Impact: Awarded at Sahasak Nimavum 2019 and showcased at Ruhuna Innovation Exhibition

Smart Shoes for the Visually Impaired

"Step Confidently: Smart shoes that guide and protect the visually impaired with advanced sensor technology."

Assistive Tech IoT AI

These Arduino-powered smart shoes are equipped with ultrasonic sensors, vibration feedback, GPS navigation, and fall detection to assist visually impaired users. They provide real-time voice alerts and emergency notifications, enhancing safety and independence.

  • Real-time voice alerts
  • Emergency notifications
  • GPS integration

Automated Plant Watering System

"Green Thumb Tech: An automated watering system that keeps your plants thriving with minimal effort."

IoT Automation Arduino

This system automates plant care by using a soil moisture sensor to determine when watering is needed. It includes an Arduino, soil moisture sensor, water pump, relay module, and tubing, showcasing the ability to create efficient, sensor-driven solutions.
